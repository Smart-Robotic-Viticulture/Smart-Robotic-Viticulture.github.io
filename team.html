Team

We are uniquely placed in the School of Mechanical and Manufacturing Engineering at UNSW Australia, which gives access to the top engineering faculty in Australia as well as a large group of outstanding students - the future innovators of our country. The team demonstrates a combination of experience across computer vision, data mining, data visualisation and robotics through a track record of international publications.

Join us now for a discussion, brainstorm or chat over coffee by contacting the group leader, Dr. Mark Whitty (m.whitty@unsw.edu.au). Prospective students are encouraged to check this website for further information on how to apply.

Dr. Mark Whitty

Dr Mark Whitty is a Senior Lecturer in mechatronics and Program Coordinator for the Mechatronic Engineering program. His research involves image processing, indoor localisation, 3D mapping and image processing. He is a Member of the IEEE.

Dr. Hiranya Jayakody

Hiranya Jayakody obtained his bachelor's degree in Electronics Engineering from University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka in 2011, and his PhD from UNSW Australia in 2016. His PhD focused on modeling and robust fast control of different types of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles. Hiranya's role at SRV group primarily involves developing image processing and machine learning solutions for stomate detection.

Ms. Julie Tang

Julie is a Research Associate in the School of Mechanical and Manufacturing Engineering at the UNSW, Australia. Her major research interests are in agricultural robotics and remote sensing. Her area of research is in viticulture and maturity estimation technologies and she is currently working on the variable rate sprayer project.

Mr. Bolai Xin

Bolai is a PhD student in Mechatronics at UNSW Australia. His major research interests are applications of computer vision and machine learning in agriculture. His current research areas are establishment of random growing model and 3D reconstruction technology of grape bunches.

Ms. Annie Xu Wang

Annie is a PhD student in Mechatronics at UNSW Australia. Her major research interests are applications of computer vision and machine learning in agriculture. Her current research area is apple flower detection and distribution estimation.


Ms. Valerie Mengying Hu

Mengying is a PhD student in Mechatronics at UNSW Australia. Her major research interests are applications of computer vision and machine learning in agriculture. Her current research area is apple tree canopy measurement.

Mr. Cameron Stewart

Cameron is an MRes student in the School of Computer Science and Engineering investigating methods for optimising non-canonical Hamiltonian Monte Carlo for efficiently sampling from probability distributions.
