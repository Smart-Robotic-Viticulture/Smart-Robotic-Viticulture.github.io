Vine Water Stress Analysis

 

Smart-phone based measurement systems

The project aims to evaluate a range of smart phone based sensing systems to develop a cheap and easy to use vine water status monitoring app. A number of methods including near-infrared spectroscopy, stereo imaging, microscopy, and thermal imaging is compared against standard water status measures such as stem water potential and stomatal conductance analysis. Based on the results from the initial investigation, the most promising analysis methods will be integrated into a single mobile application for easy hand-held field deployment and use.

  • Chief Investigator: Paul Petrie (SARDI/AWRI)
  • Project Manager: Mark Skewes (SARDI)
  • Researchers: Stanley Lam, Scarlet Liu, Mark Whitty (UNSW)
  • Fieldwork: Mickey Wang (SARDI)

 

Near-infrared Spectroscopy

Reflectance analysis to determine the relationship between water status and reflectance spectra using a SCiO Pocket Spectrometer connected to a smart-phone via a Bluetooth connection.

Thermal Imaging

Canopy temperature analysis to determine the Crop Water Stress Index (CWSI) using a FLIR One thermal camera connected to a smart-phone via a USB connection.

Microscopy

Stomata identification and stomatal aperture per unit area analysis using a hand-held ProScope Microscope connected to a smart-phone via a USB connection.

Stereo Imaging

Stereographic analysis of leaf geometry and deformation using a GoPro Dual Hero 3D camera connected to a smart-phone via a WiFI connection.
